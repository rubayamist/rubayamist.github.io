About

From soil to silicon

Rubaya Tamanna is an M.S. candidate in the Department of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) at Mawlana Bhashani Science and Technology University (MBSTU), Bangladesh. Her research sits at the intersection of two fields she has come to know deeply: artificial intelligence and geotechnical engineering, alongside a growing focus on the security of open-source software used in engineering applications.

She began as a civil engineer, earning her B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from the Military Institute of Science and Technology (MIST), Mirpur Cantonment, Dhaka, in 2017. Her undergraduate thesis examined subgrade reinforcement, exploring alternative materials for more sustainable construction.

An internship with the Bangladesh Army Corps of Engineers followed, placing her on the ground for some of the country's largest infrastructure efforts — before an interdisciplinary detour through education abroad, and a return to research driven by a clear question: how can intelligent systems make engineering safer and more sustainable?
